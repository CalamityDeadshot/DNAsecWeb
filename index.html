  1. By the principle of complementarity, mRNA is synthesized on the template DNA (transcription process).
  2. The translation process occurs in the ribosome.
    Transport RNA (tRNA) molecules, the anticodons of which are complementary to the mRNA codons in the ribosome, recognize the mRNA sequence.
  3. After the tRNA molecules recognize the mRNA codons, the amino acids (AA) of the corresponding tRNA are attached to each other (with the formation of a peptide bond), forming a protein chain.
